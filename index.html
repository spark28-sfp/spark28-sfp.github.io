The Sustainability Fellowship @ Punahou School

"earth, pale blue dot / we will fail you not" - Amanda Gorman, "Earthrise"

Hello! I am a rising junior and an aspiring environmental economist. At ‘Iolani, I am a member of the Science Olympiad team and serve as a leader of the Citizens’ Climate Lobby Youth Action Team. For the past two years, I have been a part of Climate Future Forum as a Youth Leader of the Climate & Economy Policy Working Group and have advocated for various bills, primarily carbon cashback and subsidies for electric vehicles. I joined the Sustainability Fellowship Program last year because I was inspired by the fellows I met from the first cohort through Climate Future Forum, and I knew that I wanted to pursue a career in sustainability but was not sure about what specific sector.

I think about sustainability in two ways. The first is simple: input >= output, which ensures that we and posterity will never run out of life-giving resources. This aligns with the UN definition of “meeting the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.” The second is slightly more abstract, and best articulated by Amanda Gorman through her poem, “Earthrise.” Gorman darts between the Earth and the stars, inviting us to look at our blue-green marble/home through the eyes of an astronaut. From this panoramic perspective we realize the fragility of our planet, its smallness. Geopolitical barriers dissolve easily (as they can barely be resolved), and we realize how little weight they truly hold. Sustainability is a sense of shared responsibility, borne from this knowledge that we are all one people, we live on a tiny rock floating in space, and we are so lucky to exist together. Sustainability is a commitment to “always choose Earth… always choose each other”
